Week 2: Passive vs Active Information Gathering

Overview of Passive Information Gathering

Passive information gathering involves collecting data without direct interaction with the target system or network. It includes methods like OSINT, using search engines, and physical observation. This approach is stealthy and minimizes the chance of detection, but may offer limited depth of information.

Overview of Active Information Gathering

Active information gathering involves direct interaction with the target, such as probing networks, social engineering, and directory scans. It provides more detailed information but has a higher risk of detection and legal implications. Active methods are typically employed after passive gathering has provided a base understanding.

Activity: Discussing Effectiveness

Activity: Which is more effective? Active or Passive? Discuss in groups the advantages and disadvantages of each method, considering factors like purpose, target security posture, legal considerations, and risk of detection. Different scenarios may require different approaches or a combination of both methods.

Recommended Books/Resources

Enhance your knowledge and skills in ethical hacking and cybersecurity with these recommended books and resources. Each book offers in-depth insights into various aspects of security, hacking techniques, and best practices.

  • Exploiting Software: How To Break Code by Gary McGraw and Greg Hoglund - A deep dive into software security from an attacker's perspective, covering various exploitation techniques.
  • Sockets, Shellcode, Porting, and Coding: Reverse Engineering Exploits and Tool Coding for Security Professionals by James C Foster - Offers insights into low-level programming, exploit writing, and reverse engineering.
  • The Hacker Playbook 3: Practical Guide to Penetration Testing by Peter Kim - A hands-on guide to penetration testing with practical strategies and techniques.
